Abstract

Explainability has always been a problem surrounding AI based technologies, such as machine learning. While data scientists may have thorough understanding in the chosen algorithms and training datasets, the output result is sometimes unclear or confusing. It is paramount for data scientists and other model users to understand why and how a model was trained from the data it was given.

VizAI project aims to create a website front end for an explainability unit where users can upload their own machine learning models and pairing datasets and have them explained. The explanation will be presented graphically for better understanding, so users can understand their models regardless of their technical abilities.

The achievement, impact and potential is tremendous. The web-based application would provide a simple, intuitive yet powerful tool that takes only a few clicks to have a machine learning model and its pairing datasets explained with annotated graphs, at a cost of less than $100 a month. This solution would help both individuals and organisations with limited budget to understand complex models and datasets faster, especially in scenarios like a global pandemic where every second and dollar counts.
